# Daily Operating Playbook

This guide is for the user who wants secondbrain to become a real operating system for attention.

## The goal

Use secondbrain to make better decisions faster, remember more context than other people, and stay proactive with customers, clients, investors, founders, and team members.

The point is not to "check another dashboard." The point is to direct your time toward the highest-leverage conversations and projects.

## The core mindset

Use secondbrain in this order:

```text
 Signals
   ->
 Interpretation
   ->
 Decision
   ->
 Action
   ->
 Follow-up
```

Most people live in the `Signals` layer only. They react to inboxes and chats. secondbrain becomes powerful when you use it to move deliberately through the whole chain.

## Your daily rhythm

## Morning: 10 to 20 minutes

Start here before opening your inbox in depth.

1. Open the [Dashboard](./03-dashboard.md).
2. Review `Do First` items.
3. Click through the top 2-5 important insights.
4. Check [Projects](./06-projects.md) for risk and momentum.
5. Check [Relationships](./04-relationships.md) for people waiting on you.

Morning workflow:

```text
 Dashboard
    |
    v
 Top open insights
    |
    v
 Project or Relationship detail
    |
    v
 Decide:
   reply now
   schedule
   delegate
   ignore
```

Question to ask:

- "What deserves attention before the world starts pulling me in random directions?"

## Midday: 5 to 10 minutes

Use secondbrain to re-center after meetings and message bursts.

Good midday checks:

- open one important project and update your mental model
- review groups that may contain hidden opportunities
- search for a topic that came up in conversation

Question to ask:

- "What has changed today that I should not lose track of?"

## End of day: 10 minutes

Use the system to close loops.

1. Re-open Dashboard.
2. Mark handled items as actioned, dismissed, or resolved.
3. Review any project whose next action is stale.
4. Review any important relationship you meant to respond to.

Question to ask:

- "If I disappeared for 48 hours, what would quietly become a problem?"

## Weekly review: 30 to 60 minutes

Once a week, do a deeper pass.

Suggested sequence:

1. review all active projects
2. review strong and moderate relationships
3. review top WhatsApp groups
4. refresh research on 2-5 people
5. archive dead projects
6. fix obviously wrong summaries or tags

Weekly review map:

```text
 Projects
   ->
 Relationships
   ->
 Groups
   ->
 Research refresh
   ->
 Cleanup
```

## Monthly review: 45 to 90 minutes

This is where secondbrain becomes sharper over time.

Monthly tasks:

- clean up duplicate or badly named projects
- fix important contact summaries
- rethink your tagging system
- look for repeated themes in Search
- decide whether your agent setup needs tuning

## A practical "superhuman" daily loop

```text
 1. Find the 3 people who matter most today
 2. Find the 3 projects that can drift if ignored
 3. Find the 1 overlooked opportunity
 4. Take one concrete action on each
```

If you do this consistently, secondbrain stops being reference storage and starts becoming an execution advantage.

## What to trust most

Trust these most:

- manually corrected contact and project records
- recent communications
- open insights that match what you already know
- project activity with clear recency

Treat these as helpful but imperfect:

- AI-written summaries
- inferred roles
- inferred opportunities
- group classifications

Rule of thumb:

```text
 Raw communication = strongest evidence
 Manual edits      = strong truth
 AI summaries      = useful guess
```

## How not to misuse the system

Do not:

- read every insight as equally important
- treat all opportunities as real
- keep old insights open forever
- assume the AI understands subtle human context perfectly

Do:

- validate with context
- close loops
- correct records
- use the system to guide attention, not replace judgment

## What success looks like

You know the daily playbook is working when:

- you open the right conversation faster
- you forget fewer important details
- you follow up earlier
- you feel less reactive
- more of your day is spent on the right people and projects
