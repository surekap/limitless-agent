# Founder Workflow

This guide is for founders, CEOs, and operators running a business with customers, hiring, fundraising, internal teams, and outside stakeholders.

## What secondbrain can do for a founder

Used well, secondbrain can help you:

- keep project momentum visible
- spot customer and investor follow-ups early
- prepare for important conversations faster
- remember interpersonal context better
- detect risk before it becomes loud

## The founder problem

Founders often live inside fragmented context:

- customer emails
- investor threads
- WhatsApp chats
- team groups
- meeting transcripts

The system helps answer:

- What is slipping?
- Who needs attention?
- Which conversation matters most this week?
- Where is momentum rising or falling?

## How to think about projects as a founder

A "project" is not just a formal project plan.

It can be:

- a fundraising process
- a customer rollout
- a hiring search
- a board cycle
- a product launch
- a partnership
- a legal or finance workstream

If something has recurring communications, risk, next actions, and stakeholders, it usually belongs in your Projects view.

## A good founder morning check

```text
 Dashboard
   ->
 Projects with blockers or risk
   ->
 Key stakeholder relationships
   ->
 Important groups
```

Questions to ask:

- Which customer issue could surprise me this week?
- Which internal thread is drifting without ownership?
- Which investor, customer, or candidate expects movement from me?
- Which project has activity but no clear next action?

## How to use Projects as a founder

Open Projects when you need to shift from reactive messaging into operating mode.

Use it to:

- review active workstreams
- update next actions manually
- archive false positives
- inspect communications behind a risky project

A strong founder habit:

- if a project matters, make sure its `next action` is clear

## How to use Relationships as a founder

Your business often compounds through people, not just tasks.

Use Relationships to stay warm with:

- top customers
- investors
- candidates
- senior team members
- partners
- helpful operators

Useful pattern:

```text
 Open person
    |
    v
 Read summary
    |
    v
 Read recent communications
    |
    v
 Decide:
   follow up
   prepare
   delegate
   leave alone
```

## How to use Groups as a founder

Many important signals first appear in WhatsApp groups:

- team morale
- customer urgency
- investor curiosity
- partner responsiveness
- internal confusion

Use Groups to understand:

- where you are leading
- where you are only receiving updates
- where you should intervene
- where a hidden opportunity may be forming

## Weekly founder review

Once a week:

1. review all active and at-risk projects
2. review strong customer and investor relationships
3. review key team or leadership groups
4. refresh important research profiles
5. clean up wrong summaries and stale next actions

## How this makes you look superhuman

You become:

- unusually prepared
- harder to surprise
- faster to follow up
- better at context switching
- better at remembering relationship nuance

People experience this as:

- "You remembered that."
- "You already knew the context."
- "You followed up before I had to remind you."

That is the real value.

## Limits to remember

secondbrain can help you notice patterns, but it cannot decide leadership judgment for you.

Validate before acting on:

- sensitive people decisions
- escalations
- customer risk
- hiring conclusions
- investor interpretations
