# Investor Workflow

This guide is for investors managing founders, LPs, co-investors, prospects, portfolio support, and a large web of weak and strong ties.

## What secondbrain can do for an investor

secondbrain can help you:

- remember people and context across many conversations
- prepare for founder and LP meetings faster
- see portfolio and relationship signals earlier
- find follow-ups that would otherwise get lost
- spot who is warming up, who is cooling down, and who needs help

## The investor problem

Investors often lose leverage through fragmentation:

- founders in email
- warm intros in WhatsApp
- meeting notes in transcripts
- LP discussions across multiple channels
- portfolio support spread across many lightweight conversations

secondbrain helps turn that sprawl into a system.

## How to think about projects as an investor

Projects are not only internal tasks. They can also represent:

- a live deal
- a founder support thread
- a fundraising process
- a portfolio company issue
- an LP relationship thread
- a diligence process
- an event or conference strategy

If multiple communications are orbiting the same matter, treat it like a project.

## How to think about relationships as an investor

Your edge often comes from people memory and timing.

Relationships to actively manage:

- founders
- prospective founders
- LPs
- co-investors
- operators and experts
- recruiters
- bankers, lawyers, and service providers

The Relationships page should become a lightweight relationship intelligence system.

## Your daily investor workflow

```text
 Dashboard
   ->
 founders / LPs / prospects needing follow-up
   ->
 Projects with movement or risk
   ->
 Search for meeting prep or diligence context
```

Questions to ask:

- Which founder is waiting on me?
- Which LP or co-investor has gone quiet after a promising thread?
- Which portfolio issue is surfacing indirectly?
- Which opportunity should I create, not just respond to?

## How to use Relationships as an investor

Before any founder or LP call:

1. open the person
2. read the summary
3. scan recent communications
4. check opportunities
5. refresh research if the person is strategically important

This helps with:

- remembering stage and context
- recalling subtle commitments
- not mixing people up
- acting like you have perfect memory

## How to use Projects as an investor

Use Projects to track:

- live processes
- diligence streams
- portfolio company situations
- ongoing support matters

Watch especially for:

- blockers
- risks
- stale next actions
- sudden silence after strong activity

## How to use Search as an investor

Search is one of the most underused leverage tools.

Good use cases:

- find every mention of a founder or company
- search a theme like `pricing`, `burn`, `hiring`, or `enterprise`
- pull together context before making an intro
- find old discussions that matter again now

## Weekly investor review

Once a week:

1. review all active founder-related projects
2. review key LP and founder relationships
3. refresh the research on a few important people
4. search repeated themes across the last week
5. identify intros you should make

## Turning the system into an edge

The real investor edge is not only better memory. It is better pattern recognition.

secondbrain helps when you use it to ask:

- Which founder needs help before asking?
- Which company theme keeps recurring?
- Which LP should hear from me this week?
- Which relationship has enough warmth to justify a valuable introduction?

## What makes you look exceptional

When used well, people experience you as:

- deeply prepared
- fast to recall context
- thoughtful in introductions
- proactive rather than reactive
- attentive without seeming scattered

That is a meaningful competitive advantage.
