# Client Relationship Workflow

This guide is for business owners and operators who serve customers or clients and want stronger retention, responsiveness, and account memory.

## What secondbrain can do in a client business

It can help you:

- remember every account's context
- spot churn risk earlier
- find unclosed loops
- identify upsell and referral opportunities
- prepare for reviews and check-ins faster

## The client-service problem

Client relationships often degrade because of small failures:

- delayed follow-up
- forgotten context
- missed commitments
- unclear ownership
- signals hidden in scattered communication

secondbrain is useful because it helps you see the relationship and the work together.

## How to model clients in the system

Think in two layers:

```text
 Relationship layer:
   the people at the client

 Project layer:
   the active work, account, deliverable, renewal, or issue
```

A single client may involve:

- one project for ongoing work
- one project for a renewal
- one project for a new proposal
- several important contacts

That is normal.

## Daily client management workflow

1. Open Dashboard.
2. Review anything urgent tied to a client project or contact.
3. Open Relationships for the clients you care about most.
4. Open Projects for any at-risk delivery stream.
5. Decide who needs a message today.

Questions to ask:

- Which client is waiting on me?
- Which account looks active but uneasy?
- Which stakeholder has not heard from us recently?
- Which issue is growing quietly?

## How to use Projects for client work

Treat Projects as the account operating layer.

A project can represent:

- ongoing delivery
- onboarding
- implementation
- renewal
- escalation
- proposal

Use manual edits to keep:

- status honest
- health honest
- next action clear

That turns Projects into a simple account management view.

## Churn risk and account risk

secondbrain cannot perfectly detect churn, but it can help surface early clues:

- repeated friction in communications
- stalled next actions
- open risks
- a cooling champion relationship
- message activity without clear progress

When you see these together, intervene early.

## Upsell and referral opportunities

Look for combinations like:

- strong relationship + active project + positive momentum
- repeated new topic in communications
- more stakeholders getting involved
- opportunities surfaced in Groups or Relationship insights

The point is not "trust the AI blindly." The point is that secondbrain can prompt you to look where opportunity may be hiding.

## Weekly client review

```text
 1. Review top client projects
 2. Review client champions and decision-makers
 3. Review at-risk accounts
 4. Search for repeating concerns
 5. Set one next action per important account
```

## Before a client call

Use the [Meeting Prep guide](./14-meeting-prep.md), but focus especially on:

- current project health
- recent communications
- unstated tension
- what the client likely wants next

## What "superhuman" looks like in client service

Your client feels that:

- you remember their business
- you know the latest context
- you follow up before they chase you
- you never ask them to repeat themselves unnecessarily

That perception compounds into trust.
