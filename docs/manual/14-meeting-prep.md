# Meeting Prep

This guide shows how to use secondbrain before a meeting so you walk in unusually prepared.

## The objective

Before a meeting, you want to know:

- who this person is in context
- what has happened recently
- what open loops exist
- what project or relationship state matters
- what you should probably ask or do

secondbrain is good at compressing that preparation time.

## The 5-minute prep workflow

```text
 Search or open person/project
          |
          v
 Read summary
          |
          v
 Read recent communications
          |
          v
 Check open insights/opportunities
          |
          v
 Decide your purpose for the meeting
```

## If the meeting is with a person

Go to [Relationships](./04-relationships.md).

Check:

- summary
- company and role
- tags
- recent communications
- opportunities
- research tab if it is an important external meeting

Ask yourself:

- What does this person care about right now?
- What did I last promise or imply?
- What relationship temperature am I walking into?

## If the meeting is about a workstream

Go to [Projects](./06-projects.md).

Check:

- status
- health
- next action
- recent communications
- blockers, risks, and decisions

Ask yourself:

- What is actually moving?
- What is stuck?
- What is the one thing this meeting should resolve?

## If the meeting concerns a WhatsApp-heavy context

Check [Groups](./05-groups.md) if the context lives in a group.

Look at:

- summary
- communication advice
- notable contacts
- relevant messages

This is especially useful when informal conversation matters as much as formal email.

## The 15-minute prep workflow for an important meeting

Use this for:

- board meetings
- investor meetings
- founder meetings
- client reviews
- partnership conversations

```text
 1. Open person in Relationships
 2. Open related project in Projects
 3. Search company/person/topic across all sources
 4. Read recent messages and insights
 5. Write your 3 desired outcomes
```

## A good meeting-prep note structure

You can use this mentally or in your own notes:

```text
 Person:
   who they are

 Context:
   what has happened recently

 Risks:
   tension, delays, ambiguity, commitments

 Opportunity:
   what good outcome is possible

 Next move:
   what I want to leave with
```

## Example prep questions by meeting type

### Founder or investor meeting

- What did we last discuss?
- What has changed since then?
- Is there any hidden ask or unspoken tension?
- Is there a useful intro, offer, or follow-up I should bring?

### Client meeting

- What has the client been signaling indirectly?
- Is the current project healthy?
- Are there unresolved issues I should address before they raise them?
- Is there a natural expansion or referral angle?

### Team meeting

- Which project is actually under stress?
- Who seems overloaded or blocked?
- What group dynamic should I remember going in?

## How this makes you look exceptional

Prepared people usually know the agenda.

Superhuman-seeming people know:

- the history
- the subtext
- the relationship temperature
- the likely next move

That is what secondbrain helps you reconstruct quickly.

## What not to do

Do not walk into a meeting based only on the summary line.

Always sanity-check with:

- recent communications
- project details
- direct evidence

Use summaries to orient yourself, not to replace context.
